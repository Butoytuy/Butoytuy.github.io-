What makes cats so special?

😴

Sleep champions

Cats sleep 12–16 hours a day, conserving energy for their natural hunting instincts.

👃

Super senses

A cat's sense of smell is 14× stronger than a human's, and their hearing detects ultrasonic sounds.

🐾

Silent hunters

Cats walk on their toes and retract their claws, letting them move almost silently.

💬

Purr therapy

Cats purr at 25–150 Hz — vibrations that can promote healing in bones and muscles.

🌙

Night vision

Cats can see in light levels 6× dimmer than what humans need, thanks to the tapetum lucidum.

🏠

Domesticated pals

Cats were first domesticated around 10,000 years ago in the Near East alongside early farmers.

10 amazing cat facts

1A group of cats is called a clowder.
2Cats have a specialized collarbone that lets them always land on their feet — the righting reflex.
3A cat's nose print is as unique as a human fingerprint.
4Cats can't taste sweetness — they lack the sweet taste receptor gene.
5The oldest known cat, Creme Puff, lived to 38 years and 3 days.
6Cats spend about 30–50% of their waking hours grooming themselves.
7A cat's whiskers are roughly as wide as its body — they use them to judge if they fit through gaps.
8Cats meow primarily to communicate with humans, not other cats.
9There are over 73 million pet cats in the United States alone.
10A cat's heart beats 140–220 times per minute — about twice as fast as a human's.
